CHAPTER 01

Signal in the Dark

Experimental draft. This chapter may change based on reader feedback.

The relay station drifted half a million kilometers off its assigned orbit, and nobody had noticed except Mara and the dust.

On her screen, the network map was a sparse constellation of green dots—colonies, freighters, mining rigs—each one pinging the relay in a slow, patient heartbeat. Traffic through the band was low. Too low.

She tapped the console, more out of habit than hope. The same thin stream of requests and acknowledgements scrolled by. Status: stable. Status: nominal. Status: alive.

The station hummed around her: fans, coolant pumps, the soft grind of aging gyros. Outside, there was nothing—no nearby ships, no moonlight, no reflected glow from any planetary disk. Just background stars and the cold noise of the universe.

Then, under the regular cadence of signals, something new blinked into the log. A packet with no routing data. No origin. No destination.

Just a timestamp that shouldn’t exist.

Received: 23:19:04 UTC, 07.14.2249

Mara frowned. The current year was 2248.
